Chechil Cheese

Chechil cheese is a salty braided cheese, typically smoked though there are variants that are not.

    To make chechil cheese at home you'll need
  • 3 kg of milk
  • 125 ml of 9% vinegar
  • 200 grams of salt
  • 1 liter of water.
    Once the ingredients are gathered do the following:
  1. Heat the milk to 40 degrees, stirring constantly. Before that, it should stand for a day in the refrigerator.
  2. Gradually pour in the vinegar, stirring. Cook for 5 minutes, gathering the curd mass into a lump with a spatula.
  3. Put in a colander. Heat the remaining whey to 70 degrees and pour into a large saucepan.
  4. Tear off a piece of cheese from the total mass and dip it into the whey for 20-40 seconds: if the cheese melts well, dip the entire cheese mass into the whey and continue working with it as if it was dough.
  5. For the brine, dissolve the salt in water.
  6. Form a bagel from the mass and start pulling it in circles. The process takes 10-12 minutes. Continue pulling until the threads are 5-8 mm long.
  7. Dip the finished cheese in cold brine for 5-7 minutes. Secure it to the handle of the pan and braid it.
